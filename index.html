About

I'm a second-year Software Engineering student at the Islamic University of Technology (IUT), currently ranked 1st in my department with a 4.00 CGPA. My work sits at the intersection of applied machine learning and systems engineering — I like problems where "correctness" isn't just a passing test case but a question of ordering, timing, and safety.

Most of what I've built has come out of hackathons and side projects rather than a lab: leading a 3-person team to build an AI-powered job platform, designing the safety architecture that stands between an agentic AI and a piece of physical hardware, and — most recently — building the annotation tooling for a small student-led research effort on how language models understand time.

I'm looking to move that last thread from a side project into real research: understanding how models represent sequence, duration, and overlap in events, and how better-labeled data can teach them to reason about time more like people do.

02

Research Interests

Temporal Reasoning in LLMs

How language models represent and reason about event order, duration, and overlap — and how labeled data shapes that ability.

Allen's Interval Algebra

Using formal interval relations (before, meets, overlaps, during) as a framework for annotating and evaluating temporal understanding.

Agentic AI Safety

Constraint layers and safety gates that sit between an AI agent's decisions and real-world or physical actions.

Applied ML Systems

From-scratch implementations of core ML/DL methods — building intuition by removing the library between me and the math.
